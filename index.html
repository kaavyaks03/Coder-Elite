Some background facts on South Korea

"A nation called South Korea, located in Eastern Asia, takes up most of the southern half of the Korean Peninsula. It shares boundaries with North Korea, the Yellow Sea, and the Sea of Japan. South Korea is well known for its tourist supremacy"

Etymology

After World War II, Korea was administratively partitioned in 1945. Since the Japanese capitulated on September 2, 1945, Korea's history officially began. The Soviet troops occupied northern Korea and U.S. troops entered the southern peninsula. In 1948, the Korean Peninsula was divided into a communist North Korea and a capitalist South Korea was formalized.

Demography

The easternmost region of Asia is home to the 750-mile-long (1,200-kilometer-long) peninsula known as South Korea. With 1,294 people per square mile (499 per square kilometre) of land, South Korea is a populated nation. Confucianism, a morality- and respect-based Chinese philosophy, had a significant impact on Korean culture.

Agriculture

Southern Korea is one of the productive nation which also culltivates Barley, wheat, soybeans, and potatoes are also cultivated, but most of the country's needs for these commodities must be imported. Livestock and dairying are also important. The top three agricultural products after rice are pork, beef, and milk..

Climate

South Korea's region is characterized by a hot, humid summer and a cold, relatively dry winter. Except for along the southern coast, the coldest average monthly temperatures in winter are well below negative.
